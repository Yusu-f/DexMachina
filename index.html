What makes a real expert?

Not a certificate. Not a degree. Not a title.

But the scars of having solved messy, real-world problems — the late nights, the impossible bugs, the broken systems, the unexpected crises, the years of intuition you can’t fake.

AI needs that kind of experience.

Because real-world data isn’t clean, structured, or polite.

It’s contradictory logs, ambiguous edge cases, conflicting human behaviour, systems that work; except on Friday nights, and failures nobody can reproduce.

Generic annotators can’t teach AI how the world actually works.

Experienced people can.

  • When inputs contradict each other
  • When the “obvious” answer is wrong
  • When context matters more than instructions
  • When edge cases raise their ugly heads
  • When humans flaunt their messy, surprising, irrational nature
